# Core Scientific Entities

## Phenomena (Scientific Targets)

- **Omission Response**: The brain's response to an expected but missing stimulus. A key biomarker for predictive coding.
- **Prediction Error**: The discrepancy between top-down expectations and bottom-up sensory input.
- **Sparse Spiking**: Highly selective and limited neuronal firing, often observed in response to specific task events (like omission).
- **Post-Omission Gain**: Facilitated or increased response to the stimulus immediately following an omission event.
- **Field Modulation**: Broad changes in local field potentials (LFPs), spectra, or population-level signals that may dissociate from single-unit spiking.

## Structural Components (Model Levels)

- **Neuron**: The fundamental biophysical unit. Incremental growth follows the  \rightarrow N+1$ law.
- **Microcircuit**: A small cluster of interacting neurons (e.g., E/I pairs, disinhibitory motifs).
- **Column**: A vertical cortical unit representing local processing across layers.
- **Hierarchy**: Multi-level organization (e.g., lower-order vs. higher-order areas) enabling top-down expectation and bottom-up error signals.
- **Biomarker Pipeline**: A standardized sequence of analysis steps to derive scientific metrics from raw simulation or empirical data.
